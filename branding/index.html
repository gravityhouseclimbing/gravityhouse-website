The Mark — What It Means
The South Coast landscape as identity
Layered ridge/mountain outlines — each a different colour, each simultaneously a climbing route, a contour line, a layer of community. The horizontal speed lines suggest movement. Organic, hand-drawn, distinctly local to the Far South Coast.

The minimal wordmark uses the mark lines + wordmark text only — no large mountain illustration. Use at 120px–320px wide. Below 120px, switch to the icon mark. The Ink background variant uses logo colour #EFE9DE on background #181714.
Usage Rules
Do
Always use official files — never recreate the mark by hand
Maintain clearspace = cap-height of "G" on all four sides
Use the monochrome version for 1-colour applications
Minimum print: 30mm wide (wordmark), 10mm (icon)
Minimum screen: 120px wide (minimal), 32px (icon)
Don't
Never recolour individual lines in the mountain mark
Never place on a busy photographic background without a container
Never stretch, rotate, skew, or add drop shadows
Never use the painted version at small sizes
Never substitute the wordmark by setting type in any font

Voice & Tone
Direct · Warm · Local · Honest — the same voice in every channel
Voice Traits
Direct
Short sentences. Active verbs. Say it once, clearly. No corporate fluff.
Do this
"300m² of bouldering. All grades. No rope required."
Not this
"Our world-class facility offers a unique opportunity to engage with bouldering in a supportive environment."
Warm
Welcoming without being gushing. "You" and "we." Celebrates the attempt, not just the send.
Do this
"Never climbed before? Good. Everyone starts somewhere."
Not this
"Join our thriving community of passionate climbers today!"
Local
References the valley, the coast, real places. Never could-be-anywhere generic.
Do this
"The valley gets 100+ rain days a year. We're building the answer."
Not this
"Serving the greater regional NSW area with premium fitness solutions."
Honest
We build in public. No hype. Transparent about costs, progress, and challenges.
Do this
"We're building this with the valley. Here's where we're at."
Not this
"Exciting major updates coming very soon! Watch this space!"
